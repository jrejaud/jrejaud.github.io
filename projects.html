My Projects

Here are projects (engineering or otherwise) I've done for work, school or because it looked neat.

Videos on this site were made with Final Cut Pro or Windows Live Movie Maker.

Robotic Prostetic Arm

My team built a three degree of freedom shoulder for a prostetic arm at Carnegie Mellon. The entire arm has 6 degrees of freedom.

The arm is controlled by motors on the back (not seen in the picture) and could ostensibly succesfully grab an object the size of a soda can.

Hologram Generator

By using a Microsoft Kinect and an LED Cube, you can point the camera at a very crude object and recreate it in 3d space!

This entire thing was wired and coded in four days for Build18, an Electrical Engineering Hackathon at Carnegie Mellon.

You can take a gander at the Wiki page.

True Cost Calculator

Final project for a course was to research the total cost for an object of our choice. For example: What is the total petrol, raw materials, energy, ect needed to acquire all the ingredients, bring them to the factory and make a candy bar?

I figured that was probably going to be super boring so I used the US government's NREL database and wrote a program in VBA to automate the entire process.

Download it, select the object you want and the program will calculate the raw materials and energy cost needed to build said object.

Roadable Aircraft

Capstone project for my Aerospace Engineering Minor was to build a roadable aircraft.

A roadable aircraft is an aircraft that can also drive around on the ground.

This aircraft had two motors inside it (one to power the prop and one to power the front wheels)

I had a lot of fun building it with my team and kept the schematics so I could build another one in the future.
