MOOCs and Tutorials

What is a MOOC? a Massive Open Online Course.

The following MOOCs and tutorials are free or pay resources that I have personally used to learn about programming, electronics, history, literature, ect!

I recommend them to anyone who wants to learn new things and skills. :)

Kahn Academy

Easy to grap videos that explain high school level math, physics, chemistry, ect. Equation-oriented, good for solving textbook problems.

Subjects: High school level Maths, Physics, Chemistry, ect.

Difficulty: Beginner

Adafruit Learning

Learn Arduino and other electronics. Sparkfun's tutorials will cover more theory, these will cover more "how to put the wire in the thing!"

Subjects: Fundamentals of Arduino, Electronics

Difficulty: Beginner, Intermediate

CrashCourse

Videos about history, science, and literature, much more conceptual than Kahn Academy. Excellent narration by the Brothers Green.

Subjects: High school level Maths, Physics, Chemistry, ect.

Difficulty: Beginner

Udacity

Interactive hard science and high/low level programming

Subjects: High school level Maths and a lot of programming

Difficulty: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced

Sparkfun

Learn the theoretical fundamentals of electronics

Subjects: Electronics

Difficulty: Beginner, Intermediate

ElectroBoom

Hands on (mildy dangerous) electronics.

Subjects: Electronics

Difficulty: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced

Lynda.com

Pay (not free) videos about basically every software or programming language. Very in depth!

Subjects: Electronics

Difficulty: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced
